  1. A place that is perfect for all the lovers of cinema and Bollywood! It is a getaway a little outside the city of Hyderabad. Being one of the most popular places to visit in Hyderabad, one can reach Ramoji Film City easily through many manys. Ramoji Film City is a fun and diverse venue where you can indulge in a number of activities ranging from film city tours to adventure sports and toy train rides to a number of gardens. S What's more, you can also stroll through a number of other sets and settings such as royal courts and other locations that have drawn inspiration from different architectures throughout the world. If you decide to visit this destination, after all, be sure to keep aside an entire day for this fun-filled location!

  2. One of the top things to do in Hyderabad is to visit the Hussain Sagar Lake. One of the romantic places to visit in Hyderabad in summers, do visit this Lake! This lake was built by Hazrat Hussain Shah Wali in 1562 and it was the primary source of water supply to the city. This man-made lake receives its water supply from the Musi river. You even have a 32 ft. tall Buddha statue that acts as the center of attraction to the lake. You can come here at night and spend great time here. Well no matter how much we say it is romantic, a lot of families come here too. This is another top thing to do in Hyderabad at night and you will love the feels

  3. The Four Minarets, also known as Charminar, is another monument built by the Qutb Shahi dynasty. There are many folklores about the true origin of this building with some suggesting that the structure was built to mark the end of an extremely deadly Cholera. Charminar is a square structure made of granite, pulverized marble, mortar and limestone and is known for its balustrades, balconies and stucco decorations. The four minarets that are 56-meters tall stand at each corner of the main structure. Each minaret has a double balcony, and there are 149 steps to reach the upper floor. There is a mosque on the top floor of the four-storied Charminar.

  4. Heaven for art lovers, Salar Jung Museum, is one of the largest museums in the world and one of the three National museums in India. It boasts of items collected from Japan, France, Germany, Nepal, China, Burma, Persia, Egypt, Europe, and North America. The museum has carvings, sculptures, textiles, paintings, clocks, carpets, weaponry, manuscripts, ceramics, furniture, and scriptures. There are a total of 38 galleries – 20 galleries on the ground floor and 18 on the first floor. Every subject has been displayed in a separate space. Some of the notable items on display are Aurangzeb’s sword, Veiled Rebecca by Giovanni Benzoni, Quran written with Gold and silver, Tipu Sultan’s wardrobe, Seated Lady by M F Hussain and many more.
